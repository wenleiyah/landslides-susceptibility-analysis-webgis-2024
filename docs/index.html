Significance of Landslides Susceptibility Map

Copy from slides

Landslides are one of the most widespread and devastating geohazards in the world. Landslide susceptibility mapping is a key preliminary step in risk mitigation strategies. Susceptibility maps depict the spatial probability levels for a certain area to be prone to landslides, based on local environmental variables. A second key step is exposure assessment which implies counting (or estimating) of people, property, systems, or other elements present in hazard zones.

Susceptibility linked to slope instabilities indicates the tendency of an area to breakdown. Susceptibility is the probability of an event happening in a specific zone, depending on the correlation of the instability-determining environmental factors with the distribution of the past movements. The moment of occurrence and potential victims and economic losses are not taken into account.

QGIS

A Free and Open Source Geographic Information System. It supports viewing, editing, printing, and analysis of geospatial data in a range of data formats. QGIS has a plugin infrastructure, and provide a lot of data processing tool like GRASS, QuickMapServices.

Plugin for semi-automatic classification with Gaussian Mixture Model, Random Forest, SVM and K-Nearest Neighbors classifiers. In our project we used Random Forest to do the classifcation.
