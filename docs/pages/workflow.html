⏵ NDVI (Normalized Difference Vegetation Index)

Normalized Difference Vegetation Index (NDVI) is an indicator of the degree of greenness of a biome, which is not a physical attribute but is widely used to monitor ecosystems.

⏵ Distance Characterization Data

usually distance data for roads, river networks, geological faults. Rivers can cause erosion at the base of landslides. Roads, especially cars and trucks traveling over them, can create loads that slopes cannot handle.

⏵ DTM (Digital Terrain Model)

Raster data representing the surface of the terrain. Not only provides elevation information, but also used to calculate slope, aspect, plan and profile curvature, slope angle.

⏵ Slope

The incline of a slope, represents how much elevation changes over a certain horizontal distance.

⏵ Aspect

Describe the direction of a slop. how much elevation changes over a certain horizontal distance.

⏵ Plan and profile curvature

Plan Curvature is the curvature of the surface in a horizontal plane. It indicates the rate of change of aspect along a contour line and affects water flow and erosion. Profile curvature is the curvature of the surface in a vertical plane. It reflects the rate of change of slope along a flow line and influences the acceleration and deceleration of water flow.

⏵ Slope Angle

The angle between the slope surface and the horizontal plane, measured in degrees. It quantifies the steepness of the slope.

⏵ Landslides Inventory

Includes the type, time of occurrence, activity status and depth of landslides, using data from the IFFI catalog, with each landslide represented as a point, surface or line. slope, aspect, plan and profile curvature, slope angle.
